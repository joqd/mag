## Who even is this guy

Hi, I'm Abolfazl. I write backend code for a living, and I promise this post has almost none of that in it. If you came here for Django tips, you're one page early — go check the [blog](/posts/), I'll wait.

Still here? Good. Grab tea. Or coffee. I don't judge, unless it's decaf, in which case I judge a little.

## The whole "started coding in 2018" thing

I picked up Python in 2018 mostly because I wanted my computer to do annoying repetitive things _for_ me instead of _to_ me. First script I ever wrote that actually worked felt like discovering fire. Second script broke everything and felt like discovering _why_ fire needs to be handled carefully.

Since then it's been Telegram bots, web crawlers, a genuinely unreasonable number of game mods, and eventually actual professional work. But honestly? The best part was never the paycheck — it's that feeling when a script that shouldn't work, works, and you don't fully understand why, and you just... accept the gift.

## Chess: my other unpaid full-time job

I love chess in a way that is not entirely healthy. I will lose a game, feel personally betrayed by my own brain, and then immediately start another one like a man with no memory and no self-respect.

![a chessboard mid-game, tension you can almost feel](/images/chess-board.png)

There's something about it that scratches the same itch as debugging — you're staring at a position, convinced there's a clean, elegant solution hiding in there, and 80% of the time the actual answer is "you just blundered your queen." Very humbling. Very on-brand for me.

## History nerd, unapologetically

I read about history the way other people binge TV shows. Ancient empires, weird forgotten kings, the pettiest political drama from a thousand years ago — all of it, I'm there. There's something comforting about it, honestly: whatever chaos is happening today, it turns out people have been dramatically messing things up for millennia. We're just continuing a very long tradition.

## Love, and the color blue

There's a whole part of my life I haven't mentioned yet, and it might be my favorite part of all.

Somewhere along the way, I fell in love. Properly, hopelessly, in-a-way-that-makes-me-smile-at-my-phone-like-an-idiot in love. She calls me "my bambi" in her phone, which I will never admit out loud makes my heart do something ridiculous every single time I see it on a notification — so I'm admitting it here instead, in writing, forever, on the internet. No takebacks.

Blue is her color. I couldn't tell you exactly when it happened, but at some point blue stopped being just a color and started being *her* — the sky on a good day, a specific hoodie, the little details I now notice everywhere without meaning to. It's a strange, quiet kind of magic, how a person can rewire the way you see the whole world without even trying.

I've debugged production systems at 2 AM, survived freelance clients with truly creative ideas about deadlines, and lost more chess games than I'll ever admit — and none of it has taught me as much about patience, joy, or just *showing up* for someone, as she has. Love, to me, isn't the dramatic movie kind. It's the boring-Tuesday kind. It's checking in, it's remembering the small things, it's choosing someone again on an ordinary day for no reason except that you want to.

So yeah. If chess is my chaotic hobby and code is my craft, she's the calm, blue-colored center that makes all the rest of it feel worth doing.

## Why this blog exists

Mostly for me, if I'm honest. A little corner of the internet where I can write about a Django migration disaster one week and my irrational hatred of losing on time in blitz chess the next, without it being weird. If you stick around for both, even better — welcome to the club, there's no dress code and the coffee's decent.

More posts coming. Some will be technical. Some, like this one, absolutely will not be. Thanks for reading this far — genuinely, that's kind of you.
